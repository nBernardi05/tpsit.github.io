The name

A personal computer (from the English language, in Italian literal "personal computer" or "Personal computer", usually abbreviated to PC) is any computer that lends itself personal use and personalization by the user in daily use. The term personal computer was born in the 1980s when the first computers were put on the market for the general public. In fact, previously computers were inaccessible to ordinary people due to their huge size and high price, and they were mainly bought by researchers or large companies. Currently, the term personal computer (PC) refers to desktop computers and laptop computers.

Costitution

A personal computer is typically built at the hardware level according to the architecture of von Neumann and is composed of a central processing unit, abbreviated to CPU (processor) and various peripherals, often to facilitate their use in a typical way user friendly. At the software level the relevant operating system implements in its user interface the so-called desk metaphor. Over time, the PC has actually supplanted the old machine from write for personal and office use thanks to word processing programs, adding numerous others functionality (e.g. calculation, graphics, design etc ...) thanks to its easy programmability...
